Breakthrough Cases

Short Circute In Heart Successfully

A young man with a rare manifestation of a common problem was put on the road to recovery by doctors at KG Healthcare Cunningham Road using advanced technology and care in the area of electrophysiology to set right abnormal electrical circuits in the young man’s heart, treating him from a near fatal condition. He was brought to Fortis Hospitals, Cunningham Road after an ECG done by his general physician that showed an abnormality in the heart. Young people often ignore telltale signs of heart trouble because of lack of awareness and put their symptoms down to exertion or acidity. The 32-year old patient, Mr. K R Choudhury, did not pay attention to the frequent episodes of rapid heart palpitations that he was experiencing since the last two years.

Doctor Successfully Treated Congenital Disorder

A team of doctors at KG Healthcare in Bannerghatta Road, Bangalore successfully treated a 2-month-old Iraqi baby who was suffering from ‘Bladder Exstrophy with episadias.’ It is a congenital condition where most of his lower urinary tract organs were formed outside his body. The complex procedure involved a multi-specialty team of doctors which took 10 hours to treat the rare condition. The team of doctors was led by Dr. Mohan Keshavamurthy, Director, Urology & Transplant Surgeon along with Dr. Mohan Puttaswamy, Reconstructive Orthopedic Surgeon and Dr. Yogesh Kumar Gupta, Consultant, Paediatric Intensive Care Unit (PICU).

Coconut Sized Tumour Removed

A doctor from KG Healthcare, Bannerghatta Road, managed to relieve a 35-year-old Wizdan Khadim, from Iraq, of a massive tumor in the head. The tumor, that was of the size of a small coconut, had led to doctors back home in Iraq giving her a survival chance post-surgery was very low.

Reconstructive Penile Surgery

A team of doctors at KG Healthcare, Cunningham Road, Bengaluru performed a rare reconstructive penile surgery on a 31 year old man from Saudi Arabia. The patient was diagnosed with Hypertrophic scar over penile shaft with infected ulcers over the scrotum. The team of doctors was led by Dr Mohan Keshavamurthy, Director, Urology & Transplant Surgeon at Fortis Hospital, Bengaluru. The patient was admitted into the hospital with complaint of swelling & wound in the scrotum and scarring of the phallus. The patient was diagnosed with Hypertrophic scar over penile shaft with infected ulcers over the scrotum. The scar was formed as a result of the wound healing process which occurs following physical damage of body tissues. These may cause serious functional and cosmetic disability.
